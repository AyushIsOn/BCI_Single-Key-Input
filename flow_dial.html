The Flow Dial

A drivable concept of a Neuralink-cursor keyboard. Never stop, never click. Move your mouse from the center toward a letter; likely next-letters grow into big easy arcs (the language model "inflates" them). You commit a letter by flowing outward through its arc. Then drift back to center for the next one.

How to drive it

  1. Rest your cursor in the center — that's home, it costs nothing.
  2. Glide outward toward the letter you want. Its arc highlights.
  3. Cross the outer ring inside that arc → the letter commits.
  4. Drift back through the center; the next likely letters are already big.

Feel for these things

  1. You never click and never have to hold still on a tiny target.
  2. After a letter, common follow-ons are huge — easy even when sloppy.
  3. Flip on tremor: big arcs still work; that's the noise-tolerance argument.
  4. Turn prediction off to feel how much the language model is carrying.
